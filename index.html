Abstract

This work introduces a conceptual framework for representing different levels of understanding in artificial systems through the concepts of "Resonance Data" and "Quantum-Inspired Resonance Computing" (QIRC).

We establish clear definitions for information (raw data without context), insight (data patterns that reveal meaningful relationships), and wisdom (deep understanding that guides decision-making).

The framework provides a mathematical and conceptual foundation for multi-level reasoning in computational systems, using structures inspired by quantum mechanics applied to classical computation.

This Work Includes:

  • Clear conceptual definitions and formal structure
  • Mathematical framework using quantum-inspired metaphors
  • Distinction between Information, Insight, and Wisdom
  • Foundation for multi-level reasoning systems
  • Explicit scope boundaries and non-claims

This Work Does NOT Claim:

  • New physical laws or quantum phenomena
  • Real quantum hardware or quantum supremacy
  • Consciousness, sentience, or autonomous intelligence
  • Specific implementations or algorithms
  • Operational system architectures
